Safe and Humane Removal Techniques

Once the assessment is complete, we implement our removal strategy. We prioritize humane methods whenever possible. If we discover a den site, particularly with young kits present, our approach changes. Removing kits from a den is a sensitive matter. If necessary, we can safely and carefully remove kits, placing them where the mother can find them and move them to a new location. Our goal is always to reunite families and minimize stress on the animals.

Simply removing the raccoon is not enough. To prevent future intrusions, we focus on excluding them from your property. Our technicians will identify and seal all potential entry points that raccoons might use to gain access to your home or structures. Beyond sealing entry points, we can provide recommendations for deterring raccoons from your property in the first place. This might include:

  • Securing trash cans with animal-proof lids.
  • Removing outdoor pet food and water sources at night.

Understanding Common Raccoon Problems and Signs You Need Professional Raccoon Removal in St. Joseph

Raccoons are incredibly adaptable creatures, and while they may appear cute from a distance, their presence around your home can quickly lead to serious issues. Being aware of the signs of their presence is the first step in addressing a potential problem. Many residents in St. Joseph, MO, unknowingly host these furry invaders until the damage or disruption becomes undeniable.

Nocturnal Noises in Attics and Walls

One of the most tell-tale signs of raccoons is unusual noises coming from your attic, walls, or chimney, particularly during the night. Raccoons are primarily nocturnal, so you'll likely hear scratching, scurrying, thumping, or even chattering and cries from young kits after dark. These sounds can be unsettling and indicate that raccoons are using your home as a den. Ignoring these noises allows the problem to escalate, potentially leading to more significant damage and the establishment of litters of young raccoons.

Damaged Trash Cans and Outdoor Structures

Raccoons are notorious for their scavenging habits. If you find your trash cans overturned, scattered, or with lids chewed, it's a strong indicator of raccoon visits. They are strong and intelligent enough to open many types of containers. Beyond trash, they can cause damage to sheds, decks, porches, and even siding as they attempt to gain access or search for food and shelter. This damage can compromise the integrity of your structures and lead to costly repairs down the line.

Concerns About Diseases and Parasites

Raccoons are known carriers of various diseases and parasites that can be harmful to humans and pets. Rabies is a significant concern, although direct transmission to humans is rare, it's crucial to exercise caution around any wild animal. Raccoon feces can contain roundworm eggs (Baylisascaris procyonis), which, if ingested, can cause severe neurological damage in humans. Additionally, raccoons can carry fleas, ticks, and other parasites that can infest your home and pets. Professional removal and cleanup ensure the safe handling of animals and thorough sanitation of affected areas, mitigating these health risks.

For other pest concerns, explore our services for bed bug extermination or flea treatment.

Unpleasant Odors

Raccoon urine and feces produce strong, unpleasant odors that can permeate your home, especially if they have been denning in your attic or walls. These odors are not only off-putting but can also attract other pests and indicate significant contamination. The longer the problem persists, the more ingrained the odors become, requiring professional cleaning and remediation. A persistent, musky smell is a definite warning sign that warrants investigation.

Gardening Problems and Digging

If you're an avid gardener in St. Joseph and find your garden beds dug up, crops damaged, or lawns disturbed, raccoons could be the culprits. They forage for insects, grubs, and fallen fruits, and your carefully tended garden can be a tempting buffet. Their digging can damage roots, disrupt plantings, and leave unsightly messes. Protecting your garden from these clever foragers often requires more than just simple barriers; professional insight into their behavior and deterrent techniques can be invaluable.

Fear for Pets and Children

Raccoons, while generally not aggressive towards humans unless cornered or threatened, can pose a risk to pets, particularly smaller animals. They may prey on cats or small dogs, or defensive encounters can lead to bites or scratches, potentially transmitting diseases. Allowing raccoons to roam freely on your property creates an unnecessary risk for your beloved pets and can also cause anxiety for parents concerned about their children's safety while playing outdoors. Removing raccoons eliminates this potential danger.

Visual Sightings of Raccoons

While obvious, seeing raccoons on your property, especially during the daytime, is a clear sign of their presence. Although they are nocturnal, hungry or sick raccoons may appear during the day. Frequent sightings suggest they view your property as a reliable food source or shelter. If you see a raccoon acting strangely, such as staggering or appearing disoriented, maintain a safe distance and contact professionals immediately, as this could indicate rabies.

Insulation Damage and Contamination

When raccoons take up residence in attics or crawl spaces, they often nest in or damage insulation. They shred or compact insulation material to create comfortable dens, reducing its effectiveness and increasing utility bills. Raccoon urine and feces contaminate insulation, creating health hazards and a strong, lingering odor. Replacing contaminated insulation is often a necessary part of the cleanup process after raccoon removal.

Entry Point Identification

Finding damaged vents, chimney caps, soffits, or holes in your roof or foundation can indicate where raccoons are gaining entry. They are strong and persistent climbers and can exploit even small weaknesses in your home's exterior. Identifying and sealing these entry points is essential to preventing future infestations. Our experts are skilled at locating these vulnerable areas that you might not even notice.
